Rob Thompson

Independent candidate - Taplow Parish Council By-election
Thursday 21st January 2016

  • Manifesto
  • Why am I standing?

As a Taplow Parish Councillor I will work to:

Preserve our beautiful parish, values and sense of community.

Bind our parish through following a democratic process.

Improve parish wide communication.

Engage residents in regular dialogue to understand real community issues.

Listen to local interest groups and support them in improving our community.

Deliver on our promises, making our community happy and proud.

"Democracy must be something more than two wolves
and a sheep voting on what to have for dinner".

James Bovard

Taplow Community.

Taplow is a beautiful Parish that stretches all the way from Odds Farm to Marsh Lane, on the way to Dorney. Having lived here for over a decade, I've noticed that the areas outside the village often get forgotten or are ignored. A good and effective Parish Council should stay connected to all it's residents and not just focus on the concerns of the few. I would really value the opportunity to work on your behalf to ensure that happens.

We stand at the gateway of a new era, with Crossrail beckoning and two significant housing developments on the way. If we are to preserve the beautiful nature of the Parish we must come together to listen to residents and protect the needs of the community as a whole.

Taplow Families.

I have lived in the Parish for 11 years along with my wife, Zoe & two children, Dallas and Billy. Did you know that nearly half of residents in Taplow are under the age of 45? It’s not quite the sleepy parish it appears! I was surprised to learn that only 2 out of 10 councillors presently fall into that demographic. Many people have told me they don’t have a voice and it’s easy to see why they feel that way. I’m delighted that both candidates in this election are family people, it’s a win for the residents of Taplow to get more widespread representation.

Taplow Skills.

In my day job, I work in central government, a Chief Technical Officer by trade I am currently employed as a Senior Civil Servant. In my role I am leading an internationally renowned project that manages change, embracing the best of new ideas, ensuring a quality service for users and getting best value for the people of Britain. I also sit on the board of a number of young companies as a non-executive director, advising on investment and supporting them in growth.

I hope you’ll agree, my professional skills could be a real asset to the needs of our Parish, especially at this time of flux. I would really value the opportunity to help manage the challenges our Parish is facing and contribute to the decisions that lie ahead.

Thank you for taking the time to visit.
Whatever you decide, I hope I have encouraged you to make your voice heard.
Don’t forget to vote on Thursday 21 January!
